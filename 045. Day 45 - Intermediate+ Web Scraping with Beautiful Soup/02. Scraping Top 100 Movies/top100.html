The New York Times list felt rather IMDb starter pack, full of good to great movies, but flattened by 500 voters. I decided to just cobble together my own top 100. The order isn't concrete but how I felt on this hot Saturday afternoon. Similar to them, this is based on US theatrical release dates, hence Beau Travail and The Virgin Suicides. (Yes I’ve seen 100/100 of the NYT list. No, I have not seen Madagascar 3, which was on Nicholas Sparks’ list.) 31/100 here are on their list, so this has 69 unique titles. Noice! Also Frederick Wiseman and Roy Andersson are my biggest blind spots. And I’d love some recs from other lists!

Also some will notice that my top 10 is different than what I posted on Instagram as a vote. Well, when you vote on these types of things sometimes you have to leave off what you know will be there (Mulholland Drive, Phantom Thread) to make room for some things that you'd love to be there that probably won't be, but just in case someone is of like mind (Marie Antoinette, Rachel Getting Married). Pay no attention to my star ratings as they grow and shrink.

I'll probably revisit some titles (ahem, Margaret) if people yell at me. But I had to figure out a way to include the best spoof of this century (Walk Hard).
